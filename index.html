Why people get depressed?

There’s no single cause of depression. It can occur for a variety of reasons and it has many different triggers.

For some people, an upsetting or stressful life event, such as bereavement, divorce, illness, redundancy and job or money worries, can be the cause.

Different causes can often combine to trigger depression. For example, you may feel low after being ill and then experience a traumatic event, such as a bereavement, which brings on depression.

Some studies have also suggested that a series of factors suchas stressful events, personality, family history, loneliness may constitute depression.

Gender, Orientation and Education

First, let’s make a plot to have a intuitive sense whether people of certain gender are more likely to suffer from depression.

“What is your gender?” 1=Male, 2=Female, 3=Other

From the plots, we can see that by gender, the other column has highest score among three types of gender. And male’s depression score is lowest.

Then let’s see the other two interesting domain, orientation and education.

“What is your sexual orientation?”, 1=Heterosexual, 2=Bisexual, 3=Homosexual, 4=Asexual, 5=Other

People who are heterosexual have lowest average depression score. And those who are bisexual have highest score. Minority groups take on more stress, are at greater risk of depression.

“How much education have you completed?”, 1=Less than high school, 2=High school, 3=University degree, 4=Graduate degree

By education, the risk of depression tends to decrease when educational attainment increases. Less educated people are exposed to higher risk of depression.
